Temporarily Leaving the Table

Whether it's to use the bathroom, take a call, or blow your nose, you may need to temporarily leave the table at some point during your meal. Learn how to be considerate towards your tablemates and server by using the common courtesies described below.

It's important to keep in mind that leaving the table for more than five minutes is generally considered impolite, especially if taking a phone call. We recommend silencing your phone before entering a restaurant, to avoid that situation altogether.

Table Setting

Pause

Positioning your utensils like so signals to your server that you're not finished eating, an will continue when you return.

Finished

Placing your utensils in the 4 o'clock position is a classic way of subtly letting your server know that you've finished your meal. Don't be surprised if they ask anyways, because servers would rather be safe than sorry when it comes to handling your plates.

Don't Like

A subtle way of letting your server know that you didn't enjoy the food is by placing the sharp end of your knife in between the prongs of your fork. Whether your server knows and understands this message is variable.

Excellent

Two parallel, horizontal utensils,similar to an equal sign, signifies that you very much enjoyed your meal.

Do's and Don'ts

I need to use the restroom.

DO say "Excuse me, please. I'll be right back," before leaving the table. Leaving without a word is rude and could be confusing. It is up to you whether you want to clarify where you're going.

I spilled something on my clothing.

DO excuse yourself to the restroom to clean off, if your napkin's not enough. If someone else spilled something on you, try not to make a fuss. Just clean it off as best you can, and move on.

I don't know where to put my napkin.

DO place your napkin temporarily on the seat of your chair. A napkin is never returned to the table until you're ready to depart from the restaurant.

I'm receiving a call on my mobile phone.

DON'T answer your phone at the table. If the call could be urgent and you feel it can't wait for later, first excuse yourself from the table, then find a secluded location to take the call.

I need to blow my nose, or I'm coughing a lot.

DON'T blow your nose at the table, or stay during a coughing fit. Nobody wants to see signs of sickness near their food. If you happen to cough or sneeze once, turn away and cover your mouth.

I feel threatened, unsafe, or uncomfortable.

DON'T subject yourself to an uncomfortable or unsafe situation for the sake of politeness. Instead, excuse yourself, then privately tell a staff member your situation and get their help to discretely leave.
